4.1 EdgeWeight and TimeDecay Functions

Every edge in this recursive graph carries a weight that determines how much of the cumulative output remains attributable to the original contributor at time t.

Formally, EdgeWeight_n(t) is defined as follows:

$$
EdgeWeight_n(t) = \frac{\text{DependencyPath}_n}{\text{TotalDependencies}} \cdot TimeDecay(t)
$$

Here, DependencyPath_n denotes the unique, technically necessary chains of knowledge, theory, or resource on which current outputs depend, while TotalDependencies aggregates all active input paths. This ensures derivative or plagiaristic claims dilute rapidly, while true technical advances retain proportional but decaying recognition.

The critical innovation is the TimeDecay function. Traditional systems—whether patent, intellectual property, or family inheritance—allow asset and privilege retention essentially in perpetuity. $$
TimeDecay(t) = \exp(-\lambda (t-t_0))
$$

where $\lambda$ defines the decay rate and $t_0$ the origination time. This mirrors physical principles, such as radioactive decay or resource dissipation, ensuring even the most foundational contributions eventually revert their full value to the public domain.

Consider a theory node introduced at $t_0=0$. After 10 time units, with $\lambda = 0.1$, its ongoing claim becomes exponentially small—preventing any class, enterprise, or lineage from extracting disproportionate StellarEq value without sustained new contributions.

Integrating EdgeWeight and TimeDecay effectively severs the possibility of cross-generational class monopoly. If the risk of adoption (software bugs, misdiagnosis) carries a 0.04 probability and a projected 1.0 × 10^9 Joules loss:

Risk = 0.04 × 1.0 × 10^9 J = 4.0 × 10^7 Joules

TheoryNode = (8.7 × 10^9 J - 3.0 × 10^8 J) / 4.0 × 10^7 J ≈ 214.25

The Dynamic Causal Ledger then apportions StellarEq credits recursively among contributors based on their EdgeWeight_n(t), integrating technical dependency, time decay, and aggregate adoption rates. For example, if the algorithm builds on two key earlier works, each with EdgeWeight_1(t) = 0.4 and EdgeWeight_2(t) = 0.2 (after TimeDecay), the contribution share among all nodes is distributed accordingly, with newer incremental contributors receiving higher shares during early adoption, but dissolving as public utility increases over time.

By resolving such calculations precisely, Ontic provides a politically neutral, cybernetically self-correcting mechanism for allocating social power and economic reward, independent of subjective status, capital stock, or majority vote. This unified StellarEq calculus underpins both everyday governance and civilization-level strategic direction.

10.3 Algorithmic Protocol Diagrams

Stepwise workflows enable computable governance and dynamic power allocation

To clarify the operational machinery of Ontic governance, this appendix provides step-by-step descriptions and schematic outlines of the main algorithmic protocols. These protocols collectively form the backbone of the Power Flow Protocol for Causal Governance (v1), translating abstract mathematical logic into concrete, auditable system flows.

1. Ontic Data Capture and Ontology Initialization:
- The system first ingests data spanning 153 diverse socio-economic and technological scenarios. Inputs are normalized via the StellarEq transformation, converting all social, economic, technological, and biological flows into equivalent solar energy units (Joules).
- An Ontic Causal Graph is constructed, layering nodes for Theory, Practice, and Economy. Each node and its edges are initialized with input and output StellarEq values, technical dependencies, and timestamps.

2. Distributed Knowledge Ledger Formation:
- All knowledge claims—whether theoretical, practical, or economic—are registered on a Distributed Knowledge Ledger.
- Each entry links recursively to its full dependency lineage in the Causal Graph, recording the fractional EdgeWeight_n(t) calculated from DependencyPath/TotalDependencies × TimeDecay.
- New contributions instantly dilute prior claimant influence, ensuring self-updating and anti-feudal progression over time.

3. Core Power Calculation Workflow:
- At discrete time intervals t, power is algorithmically recomputed for each node:

   Power(t) = Contribution(t) × AdoptionRate(t)

- Contribution(t) itself is recursively defined as f(CausalGraph, t), integrating the node’s time-attenuated direct and indirect effect across the full dependency network.
- AdoptionRate(t) dynamically reflects active real-world adoption of the contribution, mined from empirical data (usage, implementation metrics, distributed votes, market penetration, etc).

4. Proposal Scoring and Risk Evaluation:
- Any new governance or technological proposal is instantiated as a TheoryNode in the Causal Graph, with a score given by:
   TheoryNode(t) = NetEnergyGain(t) / Risk(t)
- NetEnergyGain(t): Computed as the difference between projected time-aggregated output and input (in StellarEq).
- Risk(t): Calculated from real-time empirical estimates as ProbabilityOfFailure(t) × PotentialStellarLoss(t), with continuous update as more data is fed into the system.

5. Dynamic Causal Ledger and Edge Weight Updates:
- Edge Weight for each dependency is recalculated in response to new contributions:
   EdgeWeight_n(t) = (DependencyPath / TotalDependencies) × TimeDecay(t)
- The TimeDecay function exponentially diminishes the value assigned to each edge over time, mathematically preventing power persistence or hereditary passing of influence.

6. Civilization Multiplier and Evolutionary Audit:
- All major nodes (and, by aggregation, the entire system) are subjected to multiplication by CivilizationMultiplier(t):
   CivilizationMultiplier(t) = Stability(t) × HumanEmpowerment(t) × CosmicJudgement(t)
- A sharp drop in any multiplier component (e.g., loss of autonomy, stagnation, or creative inertia) triggers accelerated power and resource reallocation.

7. Distributed Model Governance Execution:
- Decisions, rights, and responsibilities are computed and issued via a Model Parliament architecture, structured as a peer-reviewed, distributed computational platform similar in logic to open-source software governance (GitHub-style merge, fork, and pull-request flows).
- Proposals are integrated, split, or decommissioned based on Dynamic Epochal Audit scores, minimizing subjective factional disputes and maximizing contribution-weighted collective action.

Schematic workflow diagrams, for design and implementation, align as follows:
- The primary flowchart commences with raw data ingest, normalizes by StellarEq, traverses causal dependencies, passes through power, risk, and multiplier audit, and cycles outputs/decisions back to the empirical ledger.
- Feedback loops ensure empirical results continually update node rankings, EdgeWeights, and CivilizationMultiplier, creating a cybernetically closed, self-recursive protocol.

This multi-phase sequence ensures Ontic’s operations remain transparent, mathematically auditable, and resistant to traditional political failures—embedding dynamic feedback as a structural safeguard for distributed, physically-grounded governance evolution.
