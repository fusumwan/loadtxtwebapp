3.2 StellarEq: Mechanism, Metric, and Applications


Solar-Equivalent Energy as the Universal Political Metric Across Different Scenarios

StellarEq (Stellar Equivalent) constitutes the foundational metric of the Ontic framework by translating all political, economic, and social values into an absolute, physically rigorous unit: Joules, mapped to solar energy equivalents. This universal translation enables complex societal phenomena—ranging from an individual’s labor to civilizational-scale projects such as interplanetary resource allocation—to be reducible to a singular, objective denominator. By design, the StellarEq establishes a dimensional bridge across the full spectrum of different application scenarios delineated in the Ontic protocol, ensuring that allocation and assessment remain mathematically consistent, scenario-agnostic, and free from subjective or cultural distortions.

The mechanism of StellarEq operates via explicit energy-mapping functions. Material flows such as food, electricity, transport, and embodied infrastructure can in principle be assigned measured or estimated energy budgets. Cognitive, monetary, legal, and institutional outputs require additional valuation functions that cannot be inferred from metabolic expenditure alone: the energy consumed by theoretical work is not equivalent to its epistemic or social value. All such mappings must therefore state system boundaries, baselines, allocation rules, error terms, and the distinction between measured energy and model-assigned consequence values.

There are relevant but non-equivalent precedents. Schrödinger related biological order to metabolism while writing before modern molecular genetics (Schrödinger, 1944); Prigogine analysed irreversible processes and dissipative structures in nonequilibrium physical systems (Prigogine, 1978); and Odum developed energy-systems accounting, emergy, and transformity for ecological and social analysis (Odum, 2007). In computation, Bennett distinguished logical from thermodynamic reversibility (Bennett, 1982), while information theory supplies formal measures of uncertainty and communication rather than measures of semantic or political value (Cover and Thomas, 2006). These sources establish methodological ancestry and constraints, not the validity of StellarEq’s universal conversion.

The StellarEq transformation operates as an absolute dimensional converter. Rather than computing net value at the foundational level, it strictly converts the raw, objective energy of any activity, resource, or consequence $x$ at time $t$ into the universal political currency. It is formalized as:

$$\mathrm{StellarEq}(x, t) = \mathrm{Joules}(x, t) \times \mathrm{SolarEnergyRatio}$$

Where $\mathrm{Joules}(x, t)$ represents the objective, physically measured energy of the specific activity (whether it is energy consumed as a cost or energy saved/generated as a benefit), and the $\mathrm{SolarEnergyRatio}$ is the absolute system constant that calibrates Earth-based Joules to the standardized cosmic solar equivalent. By utilizing this pure dimensional converter at the base layer, the Ontic architecture ensures that both $\mathrm{TotalOutput}(t)$ and $\mathrm{TotalInput}(t)$ can be aggregated seamlessly in universally comparable units, setting the stage for the mathematically rigorous calculation of $\mathrm{NetEnergyGain}(t)$ higher up in the causal governance circuit.

Scenario coverage is comprehensive. At the micro scale, individual decisions—such as optimizing a software algorithm or designing an energy-saving device—are converted into StellarEq absolutes to evaluate their contribution to systemic energy efficiency. At the macro scale, national or planetary strategies—say, reallocating energy infrastructure from fossil fuels to solar power, investing in educational cognitive capacity building, or executing interstellar probe missions—undergo identical StellarEq conversion. For social or political actions not directly physical, StellarEq leverages extended cybernetic mappings: social mobilization, knowledge-production, or cumulative organizational learning are assigned energy-equivalent valuations by tracking their direct and indirect impacts on overall civilizational energy efficiency or entropy reduction.

Importantly, StellarEq’s universality eliminates the historical pathologies of subjective value, fiat currency manipulation, or political rent-seeking based on identity or narrative. All rights, privileges, and power allocations in the Ontic system are computed on the basis of validated energy-contributions, ruthlessly audited and recursively updated via empirical environmental and operational data. The StellarEq mechanism therefore disallows static ownership or perpetual monopoly: all influence is time-bound, empirical, contextual, and globally cross-comparable, mathematically negating class entrenchment and the ossification of power.

Applications of StellarEq span governance, economics, legal adjudication, and evolutionary optimization. Distribution of political power, resource access, and civilizational advancement all scale commensurately with empirically registered StellarEq contributions. In effect, StellarEq transforms society into a solar-cybernetic organism, where survival, flourishing, and legitimacy are continuously recalibrated against the objective metric of cosmic resource conversion efficiency.

To summarize: StellarEq is the mechanistic and mathematical backbone of Ontic’s computable governance, collapsing ideological, social, and economic disputes into a rigorous, dimensionality-reduced energy calculus.
