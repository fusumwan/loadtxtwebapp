Collapse of Subjective Political Models
主觀政治模式的崩潰
Technological acceleration exposes fatal limits of legacy governance frameworks
科技發展暴露了傳統管理框架的嚴重缺陷。
The accelerating transformations of the 21st century—driven by artificial intelligence, pervasive automation, and the expanding horizontal reach of algorithmic systems—have brought the structural weaknesses of subjective political models into sharp relief. Institutional forms born from majority-rule democracy, capital-based governance, and the social contract are being undermined by forces they were never designed to regulate. These legacy architectures, for centuries anchored in identity, opinion, or the aggregation of private interests, face a crisis of internal logic under contemporary conditions of speed, complexity, and machine-led societal integration.
二十一世紀加速變革——由人工智能、普遍自動化以及演算法系統的擴大應用所驅動——已將主觀政治模型的結構性弱點暴露出來。基於多數決民主、資本主義治理以及社會契約的制度形式，正受到設計來規管它們的勢力所影響。這些歷史悠久的架構，長期以來以身份、觀點或個人利益的總和為基礎，正面臨在當今的快速、複雜以及由機器主導的社會整合的條件下，面臨內部邏輯危機。
First, majoritarian democracy—nominally the gold standard of political legitimacy—suffers a critical incapacity to process the knowledge, data velocity, and technical causality now at play in social systems. Voting cycles are infrequent and disconnected from real-time decision environments. Voter aggregation reduces nuanced causal knowledge to binary choices, while the computational bottleneck of mass consent precludes the dynamic reallocation of authority in response to actual contribution, as demanded by real-time cybernetic systems.
首先，普選制度——作為政治合法性的標準——在處理當今社會系統中出現的知識、數據速度和技術因果關係方面存在根本性的缺陷。選舉週期較為不頻繁，與實時決策環境脱節。選民的彙總，會將細緻的因果知識簡化為二元選擇，而大規模共識的計算瓶頸，則阻礙了根據實際貢獻動態重新分配權力的能力，這正是實時網路系統所要求的。
Second, capital-monopoly frameworks—whether capitalist market economies or their technocratic variants—derive legitimacy and power distribution from static property allocation and historic resource accumulation. In a world where capital increasingly comprises intangible code, patents, and algorithmic platforms, centralized capital flows ossify into enduring digital monopolies. This leads to accelerating wealth polarization, class entrenchment, and the emergence of a 'useless class'—millions rendered redundant by automation, whose labour and agency decouple from value creation. The result is systemic instability: asset returns accrue to inherited capital, while productive participation is locked out by access barriers coded into the very algorithms that shape the market.
其次，以資本為主的框架——無論是資本主義市場經濟還是其技術型變體——從靜態的財產分配和歷史資源的積累中獲得合法性和權力分配。在資本日益轉向無形碼、專利和演算法平台的時代，集中式的資本流會轉變成持久的數位壟斷。這導致財富日益集中，階級固化，以及「無用階層」的出現——數百萬人因自動化而變得多餘，他們的勞動和自主性與價值創造脱節。這種結果是系統的不穩定性：資產的回報歸於繼承的資本，而生產性參與則受到市場演算法中編碼的訪問障礙的限制。
Third, the social contract model—stretching back to Rousseau, Hobbes, and Locke—relies on implicit consensus among subjects of the polity. Yet as networked platforms and automated decision-making expand, the boundaries of 'the social' blur, and contractarian assumptions lose explanatory and operational power. There is no credible procedural path to social consensus when millions interact at light speed within algorithmically segmented realities, and when power is mediated not by negotiation, but by hard-coded default rules and invisible computational feedback loops.
第三，基於羅斯洛、霍布斯和洛克的社會契約理論，強調的是政權成員之間的默認共識。然而，隨著網路平台和自動決策系統的發展，"社會"的界限變得模糊，而契約論的解釋力和實用性也逐漸減弱。當數百萬人在算法劃分的現實中以極快的速度互動，且權力並非通過談判來實現，而是通過硬編碼的預設規則和隱藏的計算迴路來實現時，沒有可行的程序性途徑來達成社會共識。
Compounding these foundational deficits is the rising dominance of platform states and global digital currencies (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, corporate stablecoins). These demonstrate that political-economic power can migrate seamlessly outside the reach of territorial law and mass electoral will. Social media, fintech, and AI governance frameworks cooperate to establish governance architectures which are neither transparent nor recursively accountable to their affected populations. These systems exacerbate Hayek’s knowledge problem—central authorities are unable to compute or process the distributed, tacit facts of societal life—while simultaneously depriving citizens of meaningful input into rule formation or modification.
這些基礎性問題的加劇，是由平台國家和全球性數碼貨幣（例如：比特幣、以太坊、企業穩定貨幣）的崛起所帶來的。這顯示出政治經濟權力可以無縫地從傳統的領土法和全民選舉之外轉移。社交媒體、金融科技和人工智能的治理框架，協同合作，以建立一種既不透明，也不會對受影響的人羣產生遞迴問責制的治理架構。這些系統，加劇了海耶克的知識問題——中央權力無法計算或處理社會生活的分散、默認的事實，同時又剝奪了公民參與規則制定或修改的機會。
The combined result is a bifurcating trajectory for human societies: on one branch, the rise of digital oligarchies—algorithmically enforced class structures, where rule is exerted by capital owners via code, not consent; on the other, the prospect of algorithmic soft totalitarianism—digital communism by default, as mass populations subsist in dependency on universally distributed basic income and platform-administered services, without any mechanism for true civilizational empowerment.
綜合而言，人類社會面臨兩種可能發展途徑：一方面，數位寡頭的崛起——透過程式碼施加的階級結構，由資本所有者控制，而非通過共識；另一方面，算法式的「軟式」專制統治的可能性——即通過普遍分發的基本收入和平台提供的服務，讓大眾社會依賴，而沒有任何真正賦予社會發展的力量的機制。
